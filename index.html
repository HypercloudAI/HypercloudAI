About Hypercloud Intelligence & Ariyan Ahmed (BloxyWave)

Opens talk2.0.html (new tab)

Ariyan Ahmed (online alias BloxyWave) is the founder and lead developer of Hypercloud Intelligence. Born with a deep curiosity for computing, Ariyan began learning on his first personal computer and rapidly expanded his skills across computer science, software engineering, electronics, game development, and artificial intelligence.

At 11 years old, Ariyan has pursued a practice-focused approach: rather than only studying concepts, he builds and iterates. This hands-on philosophy led him from early experiments to creating Roblox games, and later to exploring compact, efficient AI model design and deployment.

Ariyan has used modern AI tools as accelerants for learning. Chat-based assistants helped him study faster and test ideas more quickly; these tools served as learning partners and prototyping aids while he developed his own projects and experiments.

His technical interests include research-driven model architectures, runtime and quantization optimizations, reproducible toolchains, and production-minded packaging that enables local and low-cost deployments. These goals are reflected in Hypercloud Intelligence’s focus on efficient models, transparent benchmarking, and community-friendly toolchains.

For readers who want to learn more about the field that inspired him, see AIs on Wikipedia.

There are many projects and experiments that Ariyan has worked on; this site highlights the primary models and design goals. Thank you for taking the time to read about Hypercloud Intelligence and its mission to make powerful, locally deployable AI accessible and auditable.

About

Hypercloud Intelligence is a research and engineering initiative founded and led by developer Ariyan Ahmed (online: BloxyWave). The project concentrates on compact architectures, rigorous optimization, and packaging aimed at real-world, low-cost deployments.

Engineering priorities include architecture design, quantization & pruning pipelines, runtime optimization for target platforms, and reproducible toolchains for benchmarking and deployment.

Declaration
We are working to build an AI model without the assistance of OpenAI, Gemini, or any external LLM. Our objective is to create powerful AI that is free to use and deployable locally.
Platform focus update
During early development we focused on Android. Moving forward, Hypercloud Intelligence is prioritizing Windows-only projects. This shift enables deeper integration, richer tooling, and expanded compute options for development and distribution.

Models & technical notes

Krix alpha
Extreme mobile optimization: simplified architecture, aggressive quantization, and micro-optimizations for fast cold starts. Intended for low-resource devices; capacity intentionally limited.
Cosmicify 1.0
Balanced local inference model designed for deterministic behavior and predictable resource usage. Suitable for offline productivity tools and robotics prototyping.
Cosmicify lite
Minimal footprint variant using mixed precision quantization and layer fusion to remain useful on memory-constrained devices.
Cosmicify Max
Android-focused cutdown of the Sonic architecture with selective pruning and runtime batching to maximize throughput on mobile.
Cosmicify sonic
High-performance flagship being developed from scratch with modular attention, efficient tokenization, and training recipes that prioritize parameter efficiency.
Cosmicify sonic (gpt4all)
Archived experimental branch that leveraged community models for prototyping. Valuable for historical context; not in active development.

Technical goals

  • Maximize compute efficiency per parameter.
  • Enable on-device (and now Windows-targeted) inference with low power draw.
  • Open, auditable toolchains for quantization and deployment.
  • Rigorous benchmarking and reproducible experiments.

Ethics & safety

Releases follow safety-first practices: adversarial testing, access controls, and transparency. Community contributions will be evaluated for safety and license compatibility.
